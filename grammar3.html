Тәуелдік жалғау (Possessive Suffix)

Do you want to talk about your family in Kazakh?
To do that, you need to know how to say “my mom,” “your dad,” “our brother,” and so on.
In Kazakh, this is done using тәуелдік жалғаулар – possessive endings.
They show who owns something or who something belongs to.

Possessive endings are added to nouns to show possession, just like ’s in English (“my friend’s name”).

For example:

  • Менің анам – my mom
  • Сенің әкең – your dad
  • Оның інісі – his/her younger brother

So, instead of using a separate word like “my” or “your,” in Kazakh the ownership is built into the word itself.
